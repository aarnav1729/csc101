Computer History Timeline

Abacus: 2000 BCE

The abacus, one of the earliest calculating devices, marked the beginning of human computation.

Analytical Engine Concept: 1837

Charles Babbage's design for a mechanical general-purpose computer laid the groundwork for modern computing.

Telegraph: 1837

Samuel Morse's invention revolutionized long-distance communication, influencing the development of electronic data transmission.

Tabulating Machine: 1887

Herman Hollerith's punched card system was used for the 1890 U.S. Census, foreshadowing modern data processing.

First Computer Bug: 1947

Grace Hopper found a moth causing a malfunction in the Harvard Mark II computer, coining the term "bug."

ENIAC: 1946

The Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer was one of the first general-purpose electronic digital computers

Transistor: 1947

The invention of the transistor by Bardeen, Brattain, and Shockley revolutionized electronics and computing.

UNIVAC 1: 1951

The Universal Automatic Computer I, the first commercially produced computer, was used for business and scientific applications.

FORTRAN: 1957

IBM's Fortran programming language enabled high-level programming, making it easier to write software.

Integrated Circuit: 1958

Jack Kilby and Robert Noyce independently developed the integrated circuit, a vital component in modern computers.

Spacewar: 1962

The first video game, Spacewar!, was created by Steve Russell on the PDP-1 computer..

ARPANET: 1969

The precursor to the internet, ARPANET was a pioneering network connecting computers at research institutions.

Micorprocessor: 1971

Intel introduced the 4004 microprocessor, laying the foundation for the personal computer revolution..

First Personal Computer: 1975

The Altair 8800, designed by MITS, became the first commercially successful personal computer.

Apple I: 1976

Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak launched the Apple I, marking the start of Apple Inc.

IBM PC: 1981

IBM's personal computer set a standard for hardware and software compatibility.

CD-ROM: 1982

The Compact Disc Read-Only Memory enabled data storage and multimedia content distribution.

Mouse: 1984

Apple's Macintosh popularized the use of the mouse for graphical user interfaces.

Windows 1.0: 1985

Microsoft's first graphical operating system provided a foundation for future Windows versions.

World Wide Web: 1989

Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web, transforming how we access and share information.

Linux: 1991

Linus Torvalds released the Linux kernel, fostering open-source software development.

Mosaic Browser: 1993

The Mosaic web browser played a crucial role in popularizing the internet.

Windows 95: 1995

Microsoft's Windows 95 introduced the Start menu and became a major milestone in OS history.

Java: 1995

Sun Microsystems introduced the Java programming language, known for its platform independence.

Google Search: 1998

Google's search engine revolutionized online information retrieval.

Bluetooth: 1999

The Bluetooth standard for wireless communication between devices simplified data exchange.

Wikipedia: 2001

Wikipedia, a collaborative online encyclopedia, transformed the way knowledge is shared.

iPhone: 2007

Apple's iPhone redefined mobile computing with a touch interface and a rich app ecosystem.

Blockchain: 2008

Satoshi Nakamoto's Bitcoin whitepaper introduced the concept of blockchain, underpinning cryptocurrencies and decentralized applications.

Chat GPT: 2022

Open AI led by Sam Altman released the first ever artifical intelligence chatbot trained on an enormous dataset upto 2021 for free use to the public.
